# 5 Powerful Negotiation Tactics From Top Dealmakers

Whether you're negotiating salary, project scope, or a business deal, these tactics will help you achieve better outcomes.

## 1. Anchoring

The first number mentioned in a negotiation significantly influences the final outcome.

**How to use it:**
- Make the first offer to set the anchor
- Research market rates beforehand
- Start with an ambitious but defensible number

**Example**: Instead of asking "what can you pay?", say "based on market research, this role pays $150k-$180k. I'd like to start at $175k."

## 2. The Flinch

Show visible surprise at the other party's offer. This subtle reaction can shift anchors.

**How to use it:**
- Pause and show genuine surprise
- Follow with: "That's lower than I expected"
- Gives you negotiating room without being aggressive

## 3. Reciprocity

People feel obligated to give back when something is given to them.

**How to use it:**
- Make a concession first (on something less important to you)
- The other party typically reciprocates
- Make this a smaller concession than what you need back

**Example**: "I can be flexible on timeline if you can adjust the budget allocation."

## 4. Scarcity

Limited time or availability increases perceived value.

**How to use it:**
- "I have another offer under consideration"
- "This price is only valid through Friday"
- Be honest - never bluff on scarcity

## 5. BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)

Know your walk-away point before negotiating.

**How to use it:**
- Define what happens if you don't reach agreement
- Use this to set minimum acceptable terms
- Gives you confidence and reduces desperation

## Salary Negotiation Example

Combining these tactics:

1. **Anchor high** - "Research shows $180k for this role"
2. **Reciprocity** - "I'm flexible on equity vesting if we can discuss base salary"
3. **Scarcity** - "I have another offer that closes Friday"
4. **BATNA** - Know your walk-away (another job, staying in current role)
5. **Flinch** - React to their counter-offer

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Revealing your BATNA
- Accepting the first offer
- Negotiating against yourself
- Getting emotional
- Ignoring non-monetary benefits
